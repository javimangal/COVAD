International Advisory Board

Amita Aggarwal
Sanjay Gandhi Postgraduate Institute of Medical Sciences, Lucknow, India
Amita Aggarwal, MD, is professor in the department of Clinical Immunology and Rheumatology at Sanjay Gandhi Postgraduate Institute of Medical Sciences, Lucknow, India. She is a clinician scientist working in the field of rheumatic diseases; her major areas of research include systemic lupus erythematosus and juvenile arthritis. She has published more than 280 peer-reviewed articles. Amita is a Fellow of the Indian Academy of Sciences and National Academy of Medical Sciences (India). She is a past president of the Indian Rheumatology Association.

Laura Coates
University of Oxford, Oxford, UK

Laura Coates, MD, PhD, is a consultant rheumatologist and NIHR clinician scientist in the Nuffield Department of Orthopaedics, Rheumatology and Musculoskeletal Sciences (NDORMS) at the University of Oxford, UK. Laura’s research focuses on psoriatic arthritis (PsA); she has been involved with multiple observational and interventional studies in PsA including early diagnosis of PsA, development of PsA-specific and validated outcome measures, and optimal treatment pathways and strategies in PsA. She is chair of the British PsA Consortium (BritPACT) and is on the steering committee of the Group for Research and Assessment of Psoriasis and Psoriatic Arthritis (GRAPPA). Laura has published peer-reviewed articles including on development of screening questionnaires to identify PsA, and outcome measures of disease activity. She is the first author of the GRAPPA/OMERACT treatment recommendations for PsA, and the TICOPA study which demonstrated the benefit of a treat to target approach in PsA.

Stanley B Cohen
University of Texas Southwestern Medical School, Dallas, TX, USA

Stanley B. Cohen, MD, is clinical professor in the Department of Internal Medicine at the University of Texas Southwestern Medical School and is in private practice at Rheumatology Associates, Dallas, TX, USA. He is Director of the Rheumatology Division at Texas Health Presbyterian Hospital in Dallas, Texas and co-Medical Director of Metroplex Clinical Research Center. Stanley has published numerous articles and medical books on rheumatic diseases. He lecturers on the subjects of research and treatment of arthritis and glucocorticoid induced osteoporosis. He was a past president of the American College of Rheumatology (ACR) and the ACR Rheumatology Research Foundation.

Nathalie Costedoat-Chalumeau
Cochin Hospital, Paris, France

Nathalie Costedoat-Chalumeau, MD, PhD, is professor of internal medicine at Cochin Hospital, France, one of the five French national centers for rare autoimmune and systemic diseases, and is a member of the Systemic Lupus Erythematosus International Collaborating Clinics (SLICC) group. She has focused her clinical research and practice for the past 15 years on systemic lupus erythematosus and antiphospholipid syndrome. Her clinical research deals primarily with hydroxychloroquine and its measurement in blood, adherence to treatment, and management of pregnancy in women with lupus and antiphospholipid syndrome. She has designed and successfully led several national and international prospective studies on lupus, including the PLUS study, the Adherence study, and the GR2 study. Nathalie is also involved with several cohorts and databases of patients with lupus, including a cohort of neonatal lupus that includes more than 250 families.

Nicola Dalbeth
University of Auckland, Auckland, New Zealand

Nicola Dalbeth, MD, is an academic rheumatologist and professor of medicine at the University of Auckland, New Zealand. As a principal investigator in the Auckland Bone and Joint Research Group she leads a program of research focused on understanding mechanisms of disease and treatment approaches for gout. She has led international initiatives to define central concepts of gout, including nomenclature of disease, outcome measures in clinical trials, and response to treatment. She served as a member of the core oversight team for the 2020 American College of Rheumatology (ACR) Gout Management Guidelines and was a steering committee member on the ACR/European League Against Rheumatism (EULAR) gout classification project. She has led phase 3 clinical trials of novel therapeutic agents for gout and has published more than 450 original peer-reviewed articles. She has led both national and international initiatives to improve gout management, particularly focused on advocacy, destigmatisation, education, and development of resources.

Oliver Distler
University of Zurich, Zurich, Switzerland

Oliver Distler, MD, is professor of rheumatology and director of the Department of Rheumatology and the Center of Experimental Rheumatology at the University of Zurich, Switzerland. His main research interest is systemic sclerosis, particularly the molecular mechanisms leading to microvascular lesions, the biological action of pro-fibrotic mediators, validation of biomarkers, and design of proof-of-concept and phase II/III trials. He has been a board member of several academic initiatives including the Scleroderma Clinical Trial Consortium and the European Scleroderma Trials and Research group (EUSTAR).

Paul Emery
University of Leeds, Leeds, UK

Paul Emery, OBE, MD, FMedSci, is the Versus Arthritis UK professor of rheumatology, director of the NIHR Biomedical Research Centre at Leeds Teaching Hospitals Trust, and Leeds Institute of Rheumatic and Musculoskeletal Medicine, University of Leeds, UK. Paul is a past president of EULAR, and was inaugural president of ISEMIR (International Society for Musculoskeletal Imaging in Rheumatology). His research interests centre around the immunopathogenesis and immunotherapy of rheumatoid arthritis, spondyloarthritis, psoriatic disease and connective tissue diseases, and he has a special interest in the factors leading to persistent inflammation. Paul was instrumental in bringing sensitive imaging (MRI/US) into rheumatology practice. He established the concept of early intervention in inflammatory arthritis, with a model for early arthritis now adopted throughout the world. He is currently focusing on the prevention of autoimmune diseases with national programmes identifying patients in the pre-clinical phase. This prevention strategy is applied not only to inflammatory arthritis but to systemic lupus erythematosus, Sjögren syndrome, scleroderma and psoriatic disease. He has published more than 1100 peer-reviewed articles.

Titilola (Lola) Falasinnu
Stanford University School of Medicine, Stanford, CA, USA

Titilola (Lola) Falasinnu, PhD, is an assistant professor of medicine (immunology and rheumatology) at the Stanford University School of Medicine, Stanford, USA. Her primary research interests focus on developing best practices for adapting electronic health records for use in epidemiological inference in rheumatology, with an emphasis on pain, disability, and function. Lola’s research involves explicating how social vulnerabilities contribute to morbidity and mortality in rheumatic diseases, including characterising ways to improve sex and race/ethnic disparities in patients with rheumatic diseases, to increase participation in research by racial minorities, and to improve the diversity of the rheumatology workforce. Lola identifies as Nigerian-Canadian and is passionate about increasing the representation of patient and clinician voices impacted by rheumatic diseases in sub-Saharan Africa.

Najia Hajjaj-Hassouni
International University of Rabat, Rabat, Morocco

Najia Hajjaj-Hassouni, MD, PhD, is professor of rheumatology at the International University of Rabat, Morocco, and is a member of the French “Académie de Médecine”, Paris. Najia is past Dean of the faculty of medicine and pharmacy, Rabat Mohammed Vth University, past president of the Moroccan Society of Rheumatology and past president of the African League of Associations for Rheumatology (AFLAR). She is particularly interested in chronic inflammatory rheumatisms and osteoporosis and has participated in several European and international research projects.

Ho-Youn Kim
The Catholic University of Korea, Seoul, Korea

Ho-Youn Kim, MD, PhD, is emeritus professor of internal medicine at The Catholic University of Korea and was the founding director of the Rheumatism Research Center of The Catholic University of Korea. Ho-Youn is a past president of the Korean Association of Immunologists, the Korean College of Rheumatology (KCR), and the Asian Pacific League of Association for Rheumatology (APLAR). His current research interests include collagen autoimmunity and the role of cytokines in systemic rheumatic diseases. He has published more than 250 research articles.

Robert Landewé
Academic Medical Center/University of Amsterdam, Amsterdam, The Netherlands

Robert Landewé, MD, PhD, is a professor in rheumatology at the Academic Medical Center/University of Amsterdam, the Netherlands, and is chair of the department of rheumatology at Zuyderland Medical Center Heerlen, the Netherlands. Currently, Robert is involved in outcome research and epidemiological research in the fields of rheumatoid arthritis and spondyloarthritis. He has extensive experience in the design and analysis of clinical trials and has a special interest in medical statistics. He has published more than 500 articles covering a variety of topics, with an emphasis on clinical epidemiology, methodology, statistics and guideline development. Recently he has developed an interest in worldwide equity-issues pertaining to rheumatic diseases, as well as overdiagnosis and overtreatment. He has led the EULAR recommendation initiatives on the management of early arthritis, the management of rheumatoid arthritis, and the measurement of disease activity in clinical trials. Robert is a previous executive committee member of OMERACT, board member and past-president of the Assessment in Spondyloarthritis International Society (ASAS), previous executive board member of the Dutch Society of Rheumatology, and executive board member of the METEOR foundation.

Zhan-Guo Li
Medical School of Peking University, Beijing, China

Zhan-Guo Li, MD, PhD, is professor of rheumatology, head of the department of Rheumatology and Immunity, director of the Center of Clinical Immunology and director of the Institute of Rheumatology and Immunology, of the Medical School of Peking University, Beijing, China. Zhan-Guo is currently president of the Clinical Immunology Association of China, honorary president of Chinese Rheumatology Association (CRA) and past president of the Asian and Pacific League Against Rheumatism (APLAR). His recent research focus is on classification criteria for early rheumatoid arthritis, and establishing a diagnostic model for Sjögren's syndrome using biomarkers. He has been involved with a novel lupus therapy using low-dose IL-2 and T cell vaccine. He has published over 500 peer-reviewed articles.

David Liew
Austin Health/University of Melbourne, Melbourne, Australia

David Liew, MBBS, is a rheumatologist and clinical pharmacologist at Austin Health, Melbourne, Australia, and at the University of Melbourne. While remaining active in rheumatological clinical practice, policy work, and clinical research, he also has an interest in academic medical media communication. David’s outlook in medicine has been critically shaped by experiences working with patient populations with unmet needs, notably as a junior doctor working in a remote Aboriginal community in northern Australia, which gave him a first-hand view into how social determinants of health can influence the care and wellness of First Nations people, even within high-income countries. David is passionate about highlighting, to a broader audience, the barriers that exist in effective health communication in low-income and middle-income countries, and the challenges in improving health outcomes in the face of structural inequity in all countries.

Puja Mehta
University College London, London, UK

Puja Mehta, MD, is a senior specialist registrar in rheumatology and is undertaking a basic science PhD in post-COVID interstitial lung disease at the Respiratory Division, University College London, London, UK. She is a strong proponent of cross-speciality working and has wide clinical practice. Puja’s main research interests are rheumatic-associated interstitial lung disease, immunotherapeutics, and immunomodulation in critical illness. She has experience in drug development and the industry-academic interface, having worked on a clinical trial in rheumatoid arthritis during a pharmaceutical industry fellowship. She is currently chair of the early career research network committee and a member of the Athena Swan committee at University College London. Puja has a strong sense of justice and is committed to addressing unmet need and inequity.

Eric Morand
Monash University, Melbourne, Australia

Eric Morand, MD, PhD, is professor of medicine and head of the School of Clinical Sciences at Monash University, as well as director of rheumatology at Monash Health, Australia. He is a physician-scientist with research interests from bench to bedside, with a focus on glucocorticoids and the measurement of patient outcomes. His laboratory research on the effects of glucocorticoid-induced proteins on the immune system led to the William E Paul Distinguished Innovator Award of the Lupus Research Alliance. He founded the Lupus Clinic at Monash, now Australia’s largest single-centre cohort, which formed the basis of the multi-centre Australian Lupus Registry. Eric founded and chairs the Asia Pacific Lupus Collaboration, which has become the largest cohort of systemic lupus erythematosus patients under longitudinal study, enabling the definition and validation of novel outcome measures for systemic lupus erythematosus research. He is a member of the Systemic Lupus Erythematosus International Collaborating Clinics (SLICC) group and has served on the board of the Australian Rheumatology Association. He has published over 200 peer-reviewed publications.

Victoria Navarro-Compán
University Hospital La Paz and IdiPaz, Madrid, Spain

Victoria Navarro-Compán, MD, PhD, is a rheumatologist and researcher at University Hospital La Paz and IdiPaz, Madrid, Spain. Her research focuses primarily on the assessment of clinical outcomes and imaging in patients with spondyloarthritis. She has published over 50 peer-reviewed articles in international journals. Victoria is a member of the ASAS (Assessment of SpondyloArthritis International Society) executive committee.

Janet Pope
University of Western Ontario, Schulich School of Medicine, Ontario, Canada

Janet Pope, MD, is a professor of medicine in the Division of Rheumatology and a professor of epidemiology and biostatistics at the University of Western Ontario, Schulich School of Medicine, London, Ontario, Canada. She is the division head in rheumatology at St. Joseph's Health Centre, London, Ontario, Canada. Her research comprises studies in scleroderma, systemic lupus erythematosus and rheumatoid arthritis, including outcome measurements, clinical trials and disease manifestations. She has published more than 450 peer-reviewed articles, including several Cochrane meta-analysis reviews. She is a founding member of the Canadian Scleroderma Research Group (CSRG) and on the scientific advisory committee for the Canadian Early Arthritis Study (CATCH).

Seza Özen
Hacettepe University Faculty of Medicine, Ankara, Turkey.

Seza Özen, MD, is professor of paediatrics and head of the department of Paediatric Rheumatology at Hacettepe University Faculty of Medicine, Ankara, Turkey. Seza’s clinical and translational research focuses on paediatric rheumatic diseases, specifically childhood vasculitides, Familial Mediterranean Fever and lupus. She is a past paediatric chair in the EULAR Executive committee and past president of the International Society for Autoinflammatory Diseases. She is a council member for the Paediatric Rheumatology INternational Trials Organisation (PRINTO), an international network which facilitates clinical trials and the use of standardised outcome measures in children with paediatric rheumatic diseases. Seza has participated in many national and international projects and has published over 500 peer-reviewed articles.

John Stone
Harvard Medical School, Boston, MA, USA

John H. Stone, MD, PhD, MPH, is a professor of medicine at Harvard Medical School and holds the Edward A. Fox Chair in Medicine at the Massachusetts General Hospital, Boston, USA. He has a career-long interest in teaching, investigation, and the clinical care of patients with multi-organ system inflammatory diseases. His work has focused on the systemic vasculitides and on IgG4-related disease (IgG4-RD). John has led a number of seminal clinical trials in granulomatosis with polyangiitis (formerly Wegener’s), ANCA-associated vasculitis, and giant cell arteritis, all of which have changed the standard of care for these conditions and provided important disease insights. His work in IgG4-RD has led to the identification of several new disease associations with IgG4-RD, including lymphoplasmacytic thoracic aortitis, eosinophilic angiocentric fibrosis, and Riedel’s thyroiditis. His group also made the novel observation that B cell depletion is associated with dramatic clinical improvement, and elucidated other critical immune pathways essential to this disease. Finally, a burgeoning research interest recent years pertains to the measurement and prevention of steroid toxicity; he organised an international group of sub-specialty experts to create a Glucocorticoid Toxicity Index (GTI), designed for use as an outcome measure in clinical trials, with versions of this instrument for use in both adult and pediatric patients.

Lai-Shan Tam
The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, China.

Lai-Shan Tam, MD, is division head and professor in the Division of Rheumatology in the Department of Medicine and Therapeutics at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK). Her research interests include premature atherosclerosis and bone loss in patients with rheumatic diseases. She is the chairperson of the international affairs committee of Asia Pacific League Against Rheumatism (APLAR), a past vice president of APLAR, and a past president of the Hong Kong Society of Rheumatology. She has served on several International Task Force subcommittees of the ACR, and the EULAR Programme Committee. She is the chairperson (Asia) of the The Study GrouP for XTrEme-CT in RA (SPECTRA), and is also a member of The Group for Research and Assessment of Psoriasis and Psoriatic Arthritis (GRAPPA). Lai-Shan has published more than 200 articles in major rheumatology journals.

Yoshiya Tanaka
University of Occupational and Environmental Health, Kitakyushu, Fukuoka, Japan

Yoshiya Tanaka, MD, PhD, is professor and chairman of the First Department of Internal Medicine, School of Medicine and Dean of the Graduate School of Medical Science at the University of Occupational and Environmental Health, Japan. He has authored and reviewed more than 600 publications in English. His research focuses on pathological analysis and development of novel treatment in systemic autoimmune diseases, rheumatic diseases and osteoporosis. He is the president of Japanese Association of Clinical Immunology and a past president of Japanese Society of Bone and Mineral Research (JSBMR). He is a director board member of Asia Pacific League of Associations for Rheumatology (APLAR), Japanese Society of Internal Medicine, Japanese College of Rheumatology (JCR), and the Japanese Society of Inflammation and Regeneration.

Peter Taylor
University of Oxford, Oxford, UK

Peter C. Taylor, MD, PhD, is the Norman Collison professor of musculoskeletal sciences at the University of Oxford, and is a fellow of St. Peter’s College, Oxford, UK. He is head of clinical sciences at the Botnar Research Centre within the Nuffield Department of Orthopaedics, Rheumatology and Musculoskeletal Sciences (NDORMS), Oxford, UK. He is medical director of the patient led-charity, National Rheumatoid Arthritis Society. Peter has specialist clinical interests in inflammatory arthritis, and he has extensive experience in clinical trial design and international leadership in studies of biologic and small molecular therapies in rheumatoid arthritis and ankylosing spondylitis, including the earliest seminal trials of anti-TNF and anti-IL-6 receptor therapy. His related research expertise includes investigation of mechanisms sustaining inflammation and development of novel outcome measurements for application in assessment of response to therapy, including ultrasonographic, PET and high-field MRI technology. His interest in novel outcome measures also includes new tools for the personalised assessment of well-being which can be used adjunctively to clinical outcome measures in informing management decisions. Peter also does experimental medicine studies using targeted therapies as probes of pathogenesis.

Kim Templeton
University of Kansas Medical Center, Kansas City, KS, USA

Kim Templeton, MD, is professor, department vice-chair for diversity, equity, and inclusion, and residency program director in the Department of Orthopaedic Surgery at the University of Kansas Medical Center, Kansas City, KS, USA. She also serves as Associate Dean for Continuing Medical Education. Kim was the first McCann Professor of Women in Medicine and Science in the US. She is a past-president of the American Medical Women’s Association (AMWA), where she developed the Association's Wellness Initiative, and a member and past chair of the AMWA sex and gender health collaborative. Kim is also past vice-chair of the American Medical Association (AMA) Women Physician Section, past chair of the AMA Orthopaedic Section, and a member of the AMA Foundation LGBTQ+ Fellowship Commission. She is a past president of the US Bone and Joint Initiative and has developed multiple public education programs to address the burden of musculoskeletal conditions, including bone health and osteoporosis and injury prevention. Kim is a member of the NIH Advisory Committee on the Research on Women's Health. Her areas of interest include issues faced by women physicians and the inclusion of sex and gender in medical education. Her work addressing the issues of women physicians extends from medical students to those nearing the end of practice and includes such topics as wellbeing and re-entry to clinical practice.

Brett Thombs
McGill University, Montreal, Quebec, Canada

Brett Thombs, PhD, is a clinical psychologist, Canada Research Chair, and professor in the Department of Psychiatry at McGill University and senior investigator at the Lady Davis Institute for Medical Research of the Jewish General Hospital, Montreal, Quebec, Canada. He is the founder and director of the Scleroderma Patient-Intervention Network (SPIN), a collaboration of over 150 investigators, health care providers, patients, and patient advocates from 9 countries and 50 systemic sclerosis expert centers. SPIN investigates patient-prioritised aspects of living with systemic sclerosis and conducts trials of programmes designed to address patient priorities. He is known for his expertise in research methods and guideline development and was the first non-physician to chair the Canadian Task Force on Preventive Health Care (2017-2021). He was one of two inaugural recipients of the McGill Principal’s Prize for Outstanding Emerging Researchers and has been awarded lifetime research awards from the Association of Rheumatology Health Professionals and the Academy of Psychosomatic Medicine. He is a Fellow of the Canadian Academy of Health Sciences and a member of the College of New Scholars, Artists and Scientists of the Royal Society of Canada. He has published over 300 peer-reviewed articles.

Filip Van den Bosch
University Hospital of Ghent University, Ghent, Belgium

Filip Van den Bosch, MD, PhD, is Head-of-Clinic and associate professor of rheumatology in the Department of Rheumatology at the University Hospital of Ghent University, Belgium; the department has a long history of performing clinical and basic research, linking inflammatory bowel disease to the different rheumatological entities belonging to the spondyloarthritis concept. Filip’s main interests are in clinical research; he has been an investigator in several international or investigator-initiated clinical trials and mechanism-of-action studies in the areas of rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis and especially the diseases belonging to the spondyloarthritis concept. He has published more than 150 peer-reviewed articles. Filip currently serves on the executive committee of the Assessment in SpondyloArthritis International Society (ASAS) and is a member of the Group for Research and Assessment of Psoriasis and Psoriatic Arthritis (GRAPPA).

Diane van der Woude
Leiden University Medical Center, Leiden, The Netherlands

Diane van der Woude, MD, PhD, holds a staff position in the department of rheumatology and is head of the rheumatology outpatient clinic at Leiden University Medical Center, the Netherlands. Her research is dedicated to the processes underlying the development of rheumatoid arthritis and is mainly focused on the risk factors and autoantibodies associated with this disease. Diane is actively involved in several international research consortia.
